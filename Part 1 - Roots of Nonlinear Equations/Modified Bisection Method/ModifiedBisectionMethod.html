Modified Bisection Method

A Numerical Method for Finding Roots of Continuous Functions

The bisection method is a well-known numerical method for finding roots of continuous functions. While the classic bisection method halves the interval [a, b] containing the root at each step, modifications can involve adjusting the step size Δx to improve efficiency or accuracy. A modified version of the bisection method using a step size Δx involves evaluating the function at regular intervals rather than strictly halving the interval. This modification can be particularly useful in optimization problems or scenarios where the function's behavior is more complex.

Efficiency: A large Δx may reduce the number of iterations but at the cost of accuracy. A small Δx increases accuracy but may require many iterations.

Accuracy: A balance is needed to ensure the method is both efficient and accurate. Choosing an appropriate Δx is crucial for the success of the bisection method.
